Chess

Chess is a strategic board game played between two players on a checkered board with 64 squares arranged in an 8Ã—8 grid. The game is played by millions of people worldwide and is recognized as a sport by the International Olympic Committee.

Chess develops critical thinking, problem-solving skills, and strategic planning. The game has a rich history dating back over 1500 years, with modern competitive chess beginning in the 19th century. Chess tournaments are held globally, and the World Chess Championship is one of the most prestigious events in the sport.

Outdoor Football

Football (also known as soccer in some countries) is a team sport played between two teams of eleven players with a spherical ball. It is the world's most popular sport, played by over 250 million players in over 200 countries.

The game is played on a rectangular field with a goal at each end. The object of the game is to score by getting the ball into the opposing goal. Players are not allowed to touch the ball with their hands or arms while it is in play, except for the goalkeepers within their penalty area. Major tournaments like the FIFA World Cup and UEFA Champions League showcase the best teams and players globally.
